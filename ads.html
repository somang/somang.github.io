Call for Participants: Study Overview

Title

d/Deaf or Hard of Hearing viewer error acceptance on Live Closed Captioning.

Study description

Hello there! I am looking into developing a new version of Closed Captioning quality evaluation software. In order to enhance and reflect the viewer's subjective assessment of the software, I am conducting user research to understand the quality assessment process by viewers who are d/Deaf or Hard of Hearing. In this study, each participant will be asked to watch a set of captioned video clips, and then to rate the quality of the CC. The study involves completing a basic demographic information questionnaire, the rating session described above, and a short semi-structured interview regarding your experience with the study. The expected total time commitment for participation is 60 minutes. You will receive a small honorarium for participating in this study ($30 CAD). If you would like to participate in the study, please fill in this form. ASL interpreters are available with notice free of charge. Your participation in the research will be of great importance to assist in changing the process of CC quality assessment for d/D/HoH individuals. Thank you for your time and participation. *There will be no in-person data collection. Instead, this study will be conducted virtually if you would like to participate in the study. Sincerely,

Instructions

In this study, subjective assessment on the quality of live Closed Captioning (CC) by Deaf or Hard of Hearing (D/HoH) viewers will be studied.
Purpose of this study is to develop a method of assessment which reflects D/HoH viewer’s opinions.
Participants will be shown a series of video clips with live captions and report any errors, they identify.
Next, you will be asked to rate the confidence level of your error detection as your rating of quality.
There are four types of possible errors that you may see:

  1. Words omitted
  2. Paraphrased caption
  3. Delay* (being slower than 3 seconds)
  4. Presentation speed (being too slow or too fast)
*The 3 seconds delay you see at the beginning of clips would be to replicate the technical delay in the real setting of live Closed Captioning.

Please watch all videos until the end otherwise, the whole study will be invalid.
If you do not have the captions loaded in the first video, then you should close the survey and restart.
Please accept apologies if the captions were not loaded in the first time.
